FAQ

  • Q. Where is The U.S. Department of Education located?
  • A. The headquarts to The Department of Education is located in Washington, D.C. This location is made up of several differnt buildings with the main building being located at Maryland Avenue. However, there are also 10 regional offices, where over 1000 employees work, located across the country.
  • Q. What does the budget of The U.S. Department of Education look like?
  • A. In 2023, the Department of Education had a budget of 102.52 Billion U.S. dollars (0.9% of the total 2023 U.S. federal budget.)
  • Q. What does the Department of Education spend its budget on?
  • A. The ED has many obligations, commonly called outlays, that it spends its budget on annually. Some of these outlays include: Federal Student Aid, Elementary and Secondary Education, Special Education and Rehablilitative Services, Postsecondary Education, and much more.
  • Q. Who is in charge of The Department of Education?
  • A. The Secretary of Education Their primarally job is both leading the department and promoting general public understanding of its mission.

Some common misconspeptions about The U.S. Department of Education include:

  • That The ED establishes schools and colleges;
  • develop curricula;
  • set requirements for enrollment and graduation;
  • determine state education standards; or
  • develop or implement testing to measure whether states are meeting their education standards

All of these are responsibilities handled by the various states and districts as well as by public and private organizations of all kinds, not by the U.S. Department of Education.
